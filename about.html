What we're about

Here at PetSeeker you can explore potential pet pals across America. Adopt don't shop drives us towards providing millions of possibilities for you and the pets.

Our outreach fo users spans from searchers to shelters and rescues. We aim to bring as many rescues to light as possible. As a rescue, you can be featured, maintain a database of current pets and connect with users who aspire to find their bestfriend.

A highly technical world allows us to increase the accessibility of connecting with these shelters as well as exhibitng their current pets ready to find a loving family!

Ready to bring home a pet?



DOGS

A few things to keep in mind and prepare before bringing home a dog or puppy!

  • Know what you're in for! With a puppy comes extreme responsibility. Ensure your schedule allows for three meals a day and twice as many potty breaks until they are housebroken. They also love to chew - especially while they are teething. Have the bones and toys needed to occupy their gums so they don't take it upon themsevles to retrieve a chew toy of, say, expensive loafers.
  • That brings us to the next thing - toys. As well as other supplies such as a leash/collar, bed, crate, food, treats and food/water dishes. Dogs in general can experience tummy issues upong food transitions if done too quickly. That said, choose dog food your pup plans to stick with for a while. Your vet can steer you in the right direction if needed.
  • Child proofing? More like PUPPY-proofing. Lid your trash cans and store the valuables such as shoes, clothes, chargers, pillows. When you're away, it is advised your pup get used to a crate to provide them with comfort and containment.
  • Lastly, the animal doctor! A vet is extremely important especially with young rescue puppies. Shelters will encourage you to take the pup to the vet as soon as possible and they may even have reccomendations. In the event that you need a vet, research your local veternarians and choose one you trust.

CATS

You may believe cats are low-maintnance, but there are still things to prep prior to their arrival!

  • You won't have a chewing problem with cats but they do love to scratch things. To save your couches and tables, invest in a cat scratching post.
  • Other goodies to make your cat feel at home are food, treats, bowls, a bed, litter box, cat litter and litter scooper. They also love toys and catnip!
  • If your cat will live part of its life outdoors, a collar and ID tag wouldn't hurt. They are pretty mindful of their location and where home is but the collar will also let others know who the cat belongs to.
  • The cat will usally run the show while attempting to make him/herself at home. Let the kitty explore. You'll soon come to realize that you are no longer in charge and never will be again.
