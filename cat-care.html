Caring for your cat.

Finding the Best Feline Friend

Cat History
Let's start by asking why cats? If dogs were historically used for hunting and protecting, what are cats for? Vermin control. Yes, like real life Tom & Jerry. Once selective breeding started to trend, natural breeds, semi-foreigns and oriental breeds began to make their place.

Things to look for
Upon cat searching, it is appealing to choose the prettiest or cutest cat you find. But do keep in mind your availabilities and animal experience. Kitten are more nergetic, reckless and more work than an easy-going, friendly adult. Cats in their adulthood have more than likely peaked their traits. That said, a friendly shelter cat will forever be friendly, however a cute and at-the-moment cuddly kitten may grow up to be spiteful, unfriendly or aggressive. Take precautions as you may.

The Recipe to a Perfect Cat Bath

Naturally, cats are fastidious groomers. But don't let that stop you from giving a dirty cat a bath! Bathing a cat may also be perscribed from a veternarian if a medical condition calls for it, so it is important to know the ropes of cat bathing.

Ingredients

  • Rubber Gloves
  • Cat Shampoo (cat, not human)
  • Gentle spray bottle
  • Large Towel
  • Cotton Balls for th ears
  • Small washcloth for the face

Instructions

  • 1. Fill the sink with about 2 or 3 inches of warm (but not hot!) water
  • 2. Wet the cat from the shoulders to the tail and apply shampoo
  • 3. Just like your own hair, lather and rinse thoroughly
  • 4. Since most cats hate having water splashed on their faces, use a damp washcloth to gently clean your cat’s head
  • 5. Use a cotton ball to clean inside the cat’s ears. Never put any kind of object (NOT a Q-Tip) in your cat’s ear
  • 6. After a thorough rinsing, lift your cat onto a large towel and fold it around him
  • 7. Rub as much water from their fur as possible
  • 8. Long-haired cats may require the use of a blow dryer, but only if the noise does not terrify them. Set it on low and see if the cat will tolerate it

Training a...cat?

Litter Box Training
You may be thinking "why would anyone train a cat?!" BUT litter box training is very important and more necessary in adult rescues.
Many, many cats are surrendered to shelters or kicked to the curb due to their soiling issues. Maybe its the act of marking their territory or maybe it's consistently just missing the box (it happens, cats can have bad aim too). First, rule out any medical complications before claiming that it is feline misbehavior.
Then comes the experimentation. There are many factors to take into account such as the litter box type (hooded or unhooded), litter scent or texture, other cats in the house that use the same litter box, the frequency it is emptied, the amount of litter...the list goes on. Test out waht works and doesn't work with your cat. If you feel that you have all factors met and/or tested, referring a specialist may be your next best bet.
