Pampering Your Pup

Finding the Perfect Pooch

Know your breeds
Before you really dive into your search, know your breeds. Rescues can have many or a few crosses or may be purebred. Either way, it's important to know the characteristics of breeds and find the one most suitable for you and your home. The American Kennel Club (AKC) has a great list of all recognized breeds and even has the capability to compare them.

The first few days
Like any major change, the first few days can be ruff (pun intended). You'll want to introduce them to the family, their surroundings and any other pets. Until you can slowly transition food brands/types (if desired), be sure to ask the shelter or foster parent what food the dog has been eating. This will be his food to start off until the transition to a new food can happen if needed.

Depending on the pup's age, housetraining and even getting used to housetraining the dog will be vital. Everytime he or she eats or drinks, go outside and be prepared for a potty. It's not garunteed that they'll take this opportunity to go to the bathroom, they may just roll around in the grass or stare of the clouds in the sky. It's a great, big world out there. That said, be alert towards their behavior. If they seem to start sniffing or squating, grab that puppy and run to the grass!!

Crate traning is also important, especially at younger ages. At night or while you are away, the crate will keep them from making a mess of things or pottying indoors. Be sure to get a crate large enough for their full grown self. You can add dividers to the kennel until they grow into it. You don't excess space in the kennel for them to turn around and potentially potty in.

See our about page for more information on how to prepare your home for a new canine!

Bathtime for Fido

Dogs can be indifferent to baths, but it's more common that they hate 'em. I mean, who wouldn't be terrified of a big porcelain box with a loud gushing waterfalls a few inches away from them? Here are some tipe that may make bathtime a bit less stressful!

You will need:
These things are good to have handy when it is bath time! Especially the treats and, you know, that voice you do everytime you see a dog.

  • Large Towel
  • Treats
  • Dog shampoo ( DOG )
  • Rubber scrubber (optional)
  • Probably peanut butter

Helpful Tips

  1. Fill the tub first. The sound of the water may scare the dog. Plus, who doesn't love a little pool?
  2. Suds!! Apply shampoo generously to the super fluffy nuggets. Create suds to pull up all the dirt and grime that lives in their coat.
  3. A detattchable showerhead is your bestfriend for rinsing, especially for larger dogs. Once you feel you have completed the scrub sesh, rinse the good boy/girl down.
  4. Through out th entire event, feed the pup treats, keep their attention. Some owners will even put peanut butter on their wall and have the dog lick it up to distract them!

Obedience Training

TRAIN YOUR DOGS
Training your dog is one of the best things you can do for him or her and others around you. You can train them at home or take them to classes. If you are unfamiliar with pooch training, it is recommended that you take them to classes to better socialize them and train yourself as well!

It is important that you let fido know who is alpha (hint: it should be you). This means being strict with them when they exhibit bad behavior no matter how cute they are. Crate training them when they are in their chew-everything phase as well as soiling indoors will be a good opportunity to introduce yourself as alpha. Some rescues may be tougher than others to train given their age or breed. Refer to professional trainers when you see fit. Training treats or even a bag of kibble will help intice the pups to obey. Rewarding them is crucial!
